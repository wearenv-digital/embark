Points to consider when embarking on commercial property development and investment in the UK

When comparing freehold commercial property for sale vs commercial properties for rent, there is a lack of restrictions in relation to the use and development of freehold business properties. However, there are still restrictions imposed by local authorities, which Embark can help you mitigate.

It is always advised to consult your local authority to establish which specific restrictions may disrupt your commercial property investment project. Failure to do so could affect the profitability of your commercial real estate through extended development duration or via penalties.

If you are planning to modify the use of your commercial property, you will need to be granted permission by the local authority. Additionally, if you are buying a freehold commercial property, you must pay higher rates of stamp duty land tax than would be required on a comparable leasehold property.

Furthermore, if you plan to rent your commercial property, you must ensure the property has an energy performance rating of E or higher and you must supply a valid energy performance certificate upon the sale of your property.

Our trusted advisors can ensure you meet all requirements regarding the commercial property investment and development process.
